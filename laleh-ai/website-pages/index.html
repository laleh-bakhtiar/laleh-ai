A late scholar’s wisdom lives on

Dr. Laleh Bakhtiar (1938-2020) was a renowned Iranian-American Scholar, lauded for Islamic spirituality and Quranic critical thinking. Over her lifetime of scholarship she had translated, written and edited more than 100 books on Islam.

Now Laleh’s three children want to fulfill their mother’s final request to share her writings and reflections with the world. Through her institute, they are creating the Laleh Knowledge Lake, so the world, particularly Muslim women in the US and abroad, can benefit from Dr. Bakhtiar’s wisdom and scholarship as her family, community, and so many others who knew her during her life.

What is the Laleh Knowledge Lake (LKL)? 

LKL is the collected body of knowledge of one of the most prolific religious scholars and practitioners, the late Dr. Bakhtiar, transformed into a searchable database.

​Containing articles, books, interviews, and recitations of the Quran as well as other Islamic poetry from her nearly five decades of learning and teachings, the LKL will perpetuate Dr. Bakhtiar's knowledge and insights into the deeply spiritual workings of the Islamic faith.

Why LKL?

​“Laleh knowledge lake (LKL) will guide people to lead a radically new life by attaining ‘spiritual’ intelligence’ which is not ‘artificial.’ An intelligence that is derived from a culturally rich, living tradition of knowledge sharing passed on by sages of wisdom and not data points of information.”​
--Daisy Khan, Executive Director of the Women's Islamic Initiative for Spirituality and Equality (WISE)
“Dr. Laleh Bakhtiar was a maverick in her scholarship and it makes sense for her daughter Davar Ardalan and her institute to be leading this effort to bring her body of work to AI. With additional funding, access to all of Dr. Laleh Bakhtiar’s work in pure text form will be possible. This will result in expanding the current searchable database into a powerful and impactful tool. Additionally, through a process of breaking down her work into smaller text-based components and labelling (tagging) them all based on a set of criteria, we can build a tool that can be used to easily ﬁlter and recommend pieces of Dr. Laleh Bakhtiar’s work to people based on what they are looking for.”
--Ismail Tigrek, Data and AI Strategist based in Tennessee

It’s about the preservation of culture and history through artificial intelligence."
--Davar Ardalan, Dr. Laleh Bakhtiar’s daughter, Founder of IVOW AI and Board Member of the Institute of Traditional Psychoethics and Guidance
